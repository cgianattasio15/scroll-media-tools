What your score means

Time to rebuild.

Most brands that score here are posting more than the ones who score at the top. Volume isn't your problem. Structure is. Nothing here takes six months to fix. The fastest 90-day turnarounds we've seen come from accounts that scored exactly where you did and rebuilt one layer at a time, in order.

  1. Rebuild your profile in one sitting. Name field, bio, link in bio. Three table-stakes fixes. This is items 1, 2, and 3. Pause everything else until this is done.
  2. Set up your DM system. Saved replies, response-time floor, escalation rule. This is item 4. The DM is the conversion surface. It deserves a system.
  3. Stop posting until 1 and 2 are done. Volume on top of a bad foundation makes the foundation problem worse. Posting resumes once the floor is in.

Solid effort, real leaks.

This is where most Cincinnati local brands sit. You're posting. You're showing up. The leaks aren't visible from inside your own account, which is why most brands here stay here for years without realizing they could move. Every fix from this score compounds.

  1. Pin the right three posts. Work, Founder, Proof. Revisit item 7. Most local brands waste pinned slots. This is a single afternoon's work that compounds for years.
  2. Audit your conversion paths. One path per post, every path actually live. Revisit item 6. Choice paralysis and broken links are the most common silent killers.
  3. Move to a deliberate 40 / 35 / 25 content mix. You're probably running 90% conversion content, which is why nothing new is finding you. Revisit item 8. The algorithm needs upstream reach signal to distribute everything else.

You're closer than you think.

The visible stuff is working. The remaining gaps are upstream of effort. They live in the systems most brands never see. Most of the growth you haven't captured yet lives in the Cincinnati layer and the measurement layer.

  1. Open the Cincinnati layer. Adjacent collabs, cultural moments, established-audience partnerships. Revisit items 10, 11, and 12. The accounts that pull ahead from your score do it on the back of local amplification, not generic content.
  2. Sharpen your metric hierarchy. Saves and shares above everything. Revisit item 9. Most accounts at your score still over-weight likes and follower count. The wins live in the signal layer.
  3. Sequence, don't pile on. Pick one layer and go deep before adding another. Most local brands at your score try to fix three things at once and learn nothing.

You're dialed in.

You're running a real Instagram operation. The next gains compound on what's already working. The leaks left at this score are subtle, usually only visible from outside. The remaining 10% lives in patterns an outsider can spot that you can't.

  1. Pressure-test your Cincinnati layer. Are your collabs actually generating audience overlap? Are your cultural-moment posts the strongest of the month? Revisit items 10, 11, and 12. This is where top-tier accounts widen the gap.
  2. Audit your DM system end-to-end. The strongest accounts lose qualified buyers at the conversion surface, not the content layer. Revisit items 4 and 6.
  3. Find a second set of eyes. At this score, gaps are subtle and rarely self-visible.
