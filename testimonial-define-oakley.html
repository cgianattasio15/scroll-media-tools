Section 02 — On-Set Setup

How to Set Up the Shot

Location

Inside the Define studio

Equipment, mirrors, the studio floor visible in the background. The energy of the space is the brand. Avoid the lobby or a blank wall.

Camera Angle

Eye-level, slightly off-axis

Founder looks slightly toward the interviewer, not directly into the lens. Feels like a real conversation — not a deposition or a corporate ad.

Framing

Medium close-up (chest to top of head)

Subject in the center 60% of the frame. Safe zone: no text or critical visuals in the bottom 20% or top 15%.

Lighting

Studio lighting or soft fill

The studio likely has good ambient lighting. Use it. Avoid harsh shadows. The founder should look energized and approachable.

Audio

Lav mic or directional mic

Studios can have echo. Test audio before the first take. Do not rely on phone audio in a large open space.

Vibe

Energetic but conversational

Match the energy of the brand — Define is high-energy but approachable. Tell the founder: "We're just having a conversation. Be yourself — that's what makes this work."

⚠️ Do NOT pre-send the questions. Brief the founder on the general topic only. The authenticity of the answers depends on them being unrehearsed.

Section 03 — Interview Prompts

The Sammy Jones 5-Part Arc Prompts

Ask prompts 2 through 5 in order, then ask prompt 1 last as a rapid-fire closer. Follow up naturally on strong answers — don't rush to the next question.

2

Arc: Context — Ask First

Set the Stakes — The Studio She'd Built

Goal: Establish that she had a real, loyal community in-studio — but that community wasn't showing up online. Sets up the gap between offline and digital.

"Take me back to before we started working together. You had a studio full of loyal members, a format that was genuinely different from anything else in Cincinnati. What was the relationship with social media like at that point? Was it reflecting what you'd actually built?"

Follow-up if needed: "Was there a gap between how good the experience was in the studio and how it looked on Instagram?"

3

Arc: Conflict — Ask Second

The Fear of Trending Content

Goal: Surface the specific tension — she wanted to grow, but was worried trending content would make the brand look cheap or unprofessional. This is a highly relatable conflict for boutique founders.

"A lot of boutique fitness founders we talk to are nervous about trending content — they don't want to look like they're chasing trends or compromising the brand. Was that ever a concern for you? Was there a moment where you thought, 'I don't want to do that kind of content'?"

Follow-up if needed: "What was the fear specifically — was it about how the brand would look, or about whether it would actually work?"

4

Arc: Turning Point — Ask Third

When Trending Content Actually Worked

Goal: A specific memory of a trending piece of content that performed — and the moment she realized it didn't compromise the brand, it amplified it.

"Was there a specific piece of content — a trending format or a reel that we tested — where you saw the numbers and thought, 'Okay, this actually works, and it still feels like us'? What was that moment like?"

Follow-up if needed: "Did it change the way you thought about what Define could do on social?"

5

Arc: Resolution — Ask Fourth

The Tangible Outcome + Emotional Shift

Goal: The hard metrics (75% views increase, 163% link tap increase) plus the emotional shift — she now sees social as a tool that works for her brand, not against it.

"What does Define's social presence look like now compared to when we started? And beyond the numbers — how has having a strategy that actually works changed the way you think about growing the studio?"

Follow-up if needed: "What would you tell another boutique fitness owner who's hesitant about social media — who thinks it's not for them?"

1

Arc: Hook — Ask LAST (Rapid-Fire)

The Attention Grabber — Captured Last, Placed First in Edit

Goal: The most natural, unguarded soundbite of the shoot. The founder is warmed up and not overthinking. This becomes the first line of the edit.

"Last thing — finish this sentence without overthinking it: 'I used to think trending content would hurt my brand. Now I know...'"

Alternative rapid-fire options: "Before Scroll, my Instagram was..." / "The thing that surprised me most about what social media can do for a fitness studio is..."

Section 04 — B-Roll Shot List

What to Capture on Set

Capture every shot on this list before wrapping. Clips must be 1–2 seconds each.

1

[Context] Wide shot of the studio floor — equipment, mirrors, the full space. Slow pan. Establishes the brand world.

2

[Context] Class in session — members moving, instructor leading, the energy of a live Rev or Body class. This is the product.

3

[Context] Founder interacting with members before or after class — the community relationship. Candid, not posed.

4

[Conflict] Founder on her phone — scrolling Instagram, looking at analytics. The "before" visual. Thoughtful expression.

5

[Turning Point] Founder filming content for a reel — setting up a shot in the studio, in creation mode. The "working with Scroll" visual.

6

[Turning Point] Screen recording of a top-performing trending reel — high view count. Ask the founder to pull it up on her phone.

7

[Resolution] Founder smiling, confident, in the studio — the "after" energy. Natural, not forced. The emotional payoff shot.

8

[Resolution] Childcare room in action — kids playing, a mom dropping off before class. This is Define's most powerful differentiator and should appear in the resolution visual.

9

[CTA] Close-up of the @defineoakley Instagram profile on a phone screen. Used at the end to visually anchor the brand handle.
