Section 01 — Format

The Format: Customer Testimonial Reel

This is a 🟢 CORE format in the Scroll Media Formats Library. It sits at the Bottom of Funnel and is designed to drive conversion through social proof. The primary metrics we are optimizing for are CTR, Link Taps, and PCR.

We are executing this in the "Accordion" Documentary Style — A-roll interview footage of the founder speaking, cut with fast-paced B-roll clips (1–2 seconds each), driven by aggressive on-screen text hooks. This is not a corporate case study. It is a native Instagram Reel that feels authentic, moves fast, and earns the stop.

Section 02 — The Storytelling Framework

The Sammy Jones 5-Part Story Arc

Every testimonial follows this exact arc. The arc is not just a structural template — it is the emotional journey the viewer takes. Each section must create a specific feeling. The interview prompts in each Client Shoot Packet are mapped directly to these five sections.

⚠️ Critical Rule: The Hook (Step 1) is captured LAST during the interview (as a rapid-fire question), but placed FIRST in the edit. Do not open with the founder introducing themselves. Open with the most compelling, surprising, or emotionally resonant statement from the entire interview.

1

Hook — 0:00 to 0:03

The Attention Grabber

The most compelling, surprising, or emotionally resonant statement from the entire interview. It must work before the viewer turns on sound — the on-screen text carries the hook. The first spoken word hits on frame one. Zero dead air.

Captured via rapid-fire prompt at the end of the interview: "Finish this sentence for me: [client-specific setup]..."

2

Context — 0:03 to 0:15

Who Is This Person & What Were the Stakes?

Establish the founder's world before Scroll Media. Not a bio — a stakes-setter. The viewer needs to understand what this person had built, what they were trying to do, and why it mattered. This is where B-roll of the business, product, or space runs heavy.

Prompt targets: "Take me back to before we started working together. You already had [specific asset]. What was the world you were operating in?"

3

Conflict — 0:15 to 0:30

The Specific Moment of Friction

Not "what was hard" — but "what was the specific moment or feeling that made you realize something had to change?" The conflict must be emotionally specific, not generic. "I felt like I was posting into the void" is better than "social media was challenging." Counter-positioning lives here.

Prompt targets: "Was there a specific moment — a post that flopped, a month where nothing moved — where you thought, 'This isn't working'?"

4

Turning Point — 0:30 to 0:45

The Specific Moment of Change

The moment the strategy clicked. Must be a specific memory — a specific post, a specific conversation, a specific number they saw for the first time. "I remember when..." is the target response. This is where B-roll of the founder in action, filming content, or engaging with the Scroll team runs.

Prompt targets: "Was there a specific piece of content, or a specific moment with our team, where you thought, 'Okay — this is actually working'?"

5

Resolution — 0:45 to 1:05

The Tangible Outcome + Emotional Transformation

Two layers: the hard metric (what the numbers show) and the emotional shift (how the founder feels about their business now vs. before). The metric goes on screen as a text overlay. The emotional shift is the final spoken line before the CTA. End on a forward-looking statement, not a backward-looking one.

Prompt targets: "What does your business look like now compared to when we started? And how has having a real social strategy changed the way you think about the future?"

Section 03 — Editing Rules

On-Screen Text, B-Roll & Audio Standards

These are non-negotiable production rules derived from Briar Cochran and Caleb Ralston's content frameworks. Apply them to every testimonial edit without exception.

T

On-Screen Text Rules

Use Instagram native fonts via the "Edits" app — this helps the algorithm categorize the content. Hook text: large, bold, high-contrast, on screen for the first 2–3 seconds. Clarifying pop-ups: appear exactly when the spoken word hits. Keep lines to 3–5 words. Never use "but" in overlays — use declarative statements. Cut every filler word ("um," "like," "you know," "kind of") from the A-roll before adding any text.

B

B-Roll Rules

B-roll is visual evidence, not filler. One B-roll shot per sentence. Maximum clip length: 1–2 seconds. Each shot must visually prove or reinforce the spoken word — not just fill time. The Client Shoot Packet provides a numbered shot list. Capture every shot on the list before wrapping.

A

Audio Rules

Use a trending instrumental track (no vocals). Implement volume ducking: music lowers when the founder speaks, swells during B-roll transitions. Cut all dead air between sentences. Think in rhythmic beats, not paragraphs. The music should feel like a pulse underneath the story, not a competing element.

C

Caption Rules

Use dynamic auto-generated captions (not static). 3–5 words per line. Position captions in the center of the frame, just below the speaker's eyes. Never place captions in the bottom 20% of the frame — they will be covered by the Instagram UI.

Z

Safe Zone Rule

Frame the subject in the center 60% of the frame. Hook text: upper-center zone. Captions: lower-center zone, below the speaker's eyes. Never place text or critical visuals in the bottom 20% or top 15% of the frame. Confirm framing before the first take.

M

Metric Overlay Rule

Every testimonial must include at least one hard metric displayed as a bold text overlay at the Resolution phase. This is the scroll-stopper. The number must be specific (e.g., "118% Increase in Interactions" — not "huge growth"). Place it as a pop-up graphic timed to the exact moment the founder mentions the result.

Section 04 — Stories Promotion

How to Promote the Post via Stories

Once the Reel is live, execute a two-frame Stories sequence the same day. This drives the post's initial engagement velocity and creates a direct conversion path to the Growth Blueprint page.

Frame Format Content Action
Frame 1 Customer Spotlight / UGC Repost Repost the Reel to Stories. Add a brief text overlay: "This is what working with Scroll Media looks like 👇" Tag the client. Drives views to the Reel, signals social proof to the Stories audience
Frame 2 Direct Response / Link Story Single frame. Copy: "Want results like this for your brand? We break down the exact framework in our Instagram Growth Blueprint." Add a prominent link sticker in the center of the frame pointing to the Growth Blueprint page. Link sticker → tools.scrollmedia.co/instagram-growth-blueprint

CTA Strategy: The caption on the Reel uses a soft DM trigger — "DM us 'BLUEPRINT' to see the exact strategy we used." This creates an inbound signal Riley can respond to manually, or that can be automated via ManyChat later. The Stories link sticker provides the direct conversion path for viewers who are ready to act immediately.

Section 05 — Quality Control

Post-Production Checklist

Before submitting any edit for approval, verify every item on this list. Do not submit an edit that fails any check.

Hook: Does the first line earn the stop? Is text on screen within the first 3 seconds? Does the first spoken word hit on frame one?

Filler Words: Have all "um," "like," "you know," and "kind of" instances been removed from the A-roll?

B-Roll Pacing: Are all B-roll clips 1–2 seconds or less? Is there one B-roll shot per sentence?

On-Screen Text: Is the hook text large, bold, and contrasting? Are clarifying pop-ups timed to the exact spoken moment? Are lines 3–5 words max?

Captions: Are captions dynamic, 3–5 words per line, and positioned within the safe zone (below the speaker's eyes, above the bottom 20%)?

Audio: Is volume ducking applied? Is the music subtle enough to not overpower the founder's voice?

Metric Overlay: Is at least one specific, tangible metric displayed as a bold text overlay at the Resolution phase?

CTA: Is the CTA clear, specific, and on screen at the end? Does it reference "DM us 'BLUEPRINT'"?

Safe Zone: Is the subject framed in the center 60%? Is no critical text placed in the bottom 20% or top 15% of the frame?

Stories: Are both Stories frames (Repost + Link Story) scheduled for the same day as the Reel goes live?
