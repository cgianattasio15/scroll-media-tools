This Week's Strategy Signals

Top 3 highest-priority signals · Week of Mar 16–Mar 22 · Use these to drive coaching conversations
🎯
Instagram Prioritizes Original Video Content
Adam Mosseri emphasized last week that Instagram is boosting native, original video content over repurposed or reposted clips to drive higher engagement and reach. Creators and brands posting unique video content see more distribution in Reels and Stories.
Why It Matters
Boutique service brands that produce authentic original videos can break through the noise and gain more organic visibility compared to those relying on curated or reposted content.
In Action — 2 Examples
Example 1
For a med spa: Create a behind-the-scenes Reel showing a day in the life of a facial treatment, focusing on real client reactions and staff expertise.
Example 2
For a fitness studio: Film original short workout tip videos featuring trainers demonstrating moves, rather than reposting generic fitness clips.
Coaching Question
"Are we developing unique, original video content this week instead of relying on reposts or generic stock clips?"
⚡
Instagram Testing Native Link Stickers in Stories
Brock Johnson revealed Instagram is expanding native link stickers to more accounts, enabling direct traffic from Stories without the need for swipe-up or bio links. This opens new paths for conversion and sales.
Why It Matters
Boutique service brands can now drive immediate bookings, purchases, or inquiries directly from Stories, streamlining the customer journey and improving measurable ROI.
In Action — 2 Examples
Example 1
For a dental clinic: Add a link sticker in Stories promoting a limited-time teeth whitening special, linking directly to the booking page.
Example 2
For a jewelry brand: Use a link sticker in Stories showcasing a new collection launch, linking straight to product pages for instant purchases.
Coaching Question
"Have we integrated native link stickers into our Stories strategy to boost direct conversions this week?"
🔑
Lucas O'Keefe Urges Focus on Community Engagement
Lucas O’Keefe highlighted that Instagram’s algorithm increasingly favors accounts that engage meaningfully with their followers through comments, DMs, and interactive story features.
Why It Matters
Service brands that actively respond and create two-way conversations build trust and stronger algorithmic favor, leading to better organic reach and client loyalty.
In Action — 2 Examples
Example 1
For a wellness center: Host a weekly Q&A in Stories using question stickers and respond personally to follower inquiries about holistic health.
Example 2
For a retail boutique: Regularly comment on followers’ posts and DMs, then share user-generated content to foster authentic community connections.
Coaching Question
"What new engagement tactics can we implement this week to increase authentic follower interaction?"
