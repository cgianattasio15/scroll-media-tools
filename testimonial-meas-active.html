Section 02 — On-Set Setup

How to Set Up the Shot

Location

Wearing MEAS product, active environment

The founder should be in MEAS activewear. Location can be a gym, outdoor space, or clean indoor environment — anywhere that reflects the active brand identity.

Camera Angle

Eye-level, slightly off-axis

Founder looks slightly toward the interviewer, not directly into the lens. Feels like a real conversation — not a corporate ad.

Framing

Medium close-up (chest to top of head)

Subject in the center 60% of the frame. Safe zone: no text or critical visuals in the bottom 20% or top 15%.

Lighting

Natural light preferred

MEAS is an active, outdoor-forward brand. Natural light fits the aesthetic. Avoid overly studio-lit setups that feel corporate.

Audio

Lav mic or directional mic

If shooting outdoors, use a lav mic to minimize wind noise. Test audio before the first take.

Vibe

Energetic, founder-led, authentic

MEAS is a founder-led brand with a strong personal identity. The founder's personality is the brand. Tell her: "Just talk to me like you'd talk to someone in your community."

⚠️ Do NOT pre-send the questions. Brief the founder on the general topic only. The authenticity of the answers depends on them being unrehearsed.

Section 03 — Interview Prompts

The Sammy Jones 5-Part Arc Prompts

Ask prompts 2 through 5 in order, then ask prompt 1 last as a rapid-fire closer. Follow up naturally on strong answers.

2

Arc: Context — Ask First

Set the Stakes — The Community She'd Already Built

Goal: Establish that she had a real, tangible offline community — ambassadors, collabs, events — before Scroll. This makes the conflict (missing digital strategy) more powerful because the foundation was already strong.

"Take me back to before we started working together. You already had a real community around MEAS — ambassadors, brand partnerships, people who genuinely loved the product. What was the business like at that point? And what was the relationship with social media?"

Follow-up if needed: "Was there a gap between how strong the community felt in real life and how it looked on Instagram?"

3

Arc: Conflict — Ask Second

The Specific Gap — Offline Strength, Digital Weakness

Goal: The specific frustration of having a strong real-world brand that wasn't translating online. Not "social was hard" — but "I had something real and I couldn't show it."

"Was there a specific moment where you felt the frustration of that gap — like you had this real community, these real partnerships, and Instagram just wasn't capturing it? What did that feel like?"

Follow-up if needed: "Was it more frustrating because you knew the brand was good — it just wasn't showing up that way online?"

4

Arc: Turning Point — Ask Third

When the Digital Strategy Matched the Offline Brand

Goal: The specific moment when the social strategy started to reflect the real brand — when the ambassador program, the collabs, the community energy started showing up in the content and the numbers.

"Was there a specific piece of content — or a specific moment — where you saw the social strategy start to match what you'd built offline? Where Instagram finally started to feel like MEAS?"

Follow-up if needed: "Was it a specific post that performed, or more of a gradual shift where you started seeing the numbers move?"

5

Arc: Resolution — Ask Fourth

The Tangible Outcome + Emotional Shift

Goal: The hard metrics (118% interaction increase, 70% awareness growth in 90 days) plus the emotional transformation — she now has a digital presence that matches the brand she's built.

"What does MEAS Active's social presence look like now compared to when we started? And beyond the numbers — how has having a real digital strategy changed the way you think about growing the brand?"

Follow-up if needed: "What would you tell another founder who has a strong product and a strong community but is struggling to translate that online?"

1

Arc: Hook — Ask LAST (Rapid-Fire)

The Attention Grabber — Captured Last, Placed First in Edit

Goal: The most natural, unguarded soundbite of the shoot. The founder is warmed up and not overthinking. This becomes the first line of the edit.

"Last thing — finish this sentence without overthinking it: 'I had the community. What I didn't have was...'"

Alternative rapid-fire options: "In 90 days, MEAS went from..." / "The thing that changed everything for us was..."
