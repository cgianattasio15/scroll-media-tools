The 7-Second
Impression Test

Does your Instagram profile pass the test — or is it losing you clients before they even scroll?

27 Checkpoints 4 Sections ~10 min to complete Free from Scroll Media

You have 7 seconds to make a first impression.

That's how long a new visitor spends on your Instagram profile before they decide to follow you — or leave forever.

In those 7 seconds, they are asking three questions:

Who are you? What do you do? Why should I trust you?

If your profile doesn't answer these questions clearly and instantly, you're losing potential clients every single day — no matter how good your content is.

This scorecard gives you an honest, objective look at your profile through the eyes of a potential client. Use it to find your highest-leverage gaps.

How to Use This Scorecard

  1. Be honest. The goal isn't a perfect score — it's finding your biggest opportunity.
  2. Check each box for every item your profile currently has in place.
  3. Tally your score at the end to see where you stand and what to do next.
Section 01

Profile & First Impressions

The core elements a new visitor sees in the first 7 seconds.

  • 01
    Optimized Handle
    Your username is simple, memorable, and easy to search — no confusing numbers or symbols.
  • 02
    High-Quality Profile Photo
    A clear, professional headshot (for a personal brand) or a clean, legible logo (for a business brand).
  • 03
    Keyword-Optimized Name Field
    Your "Name" field includes what you do and for whom — not just your name. (e.g., "Dr. Jane Smith | Austin Med Spa")
  • 04
    Clear & Compelling Bio
    Your bio clearly states who you help, what problem you solve, and what makes you different — in 150 characters or less.
  • 05
    Social Proof Element
    Your bio includes a credible form of social proof. (e.g., "100+ 5-Star Reviews," "As seen in Forbes," "Trusted by 50+ clients")
  • 06
    Singular, Clear CTA
    Your bio has one primary call to action that is easy to understand and points to a single destination.
  • 07
    Strategic Link-in-Bio
    Your link goes directly to a high-value destination (booking page, product, application) — not a generic Linktree.
Section 02

Content & Storytelling

How well your content communicates authority, value, and trust.

  • 08
    Defined Content Pillars
    You have 3–5 clear themes you talk about consistently. A new visitor can identify your focus immediately.
  • 09
    Clear Ideal Viewer Profile
    It's obvious from your content exactly who you're trying to reach. Your audience self-identifies immediately.
  • 10
    Value-Driven Content Mix
    Your content educates, entertains, or inspires your audience — not just sells to them. At least 70% is value-first.
  • 11
    Consistent Visual Identity
    Your posts have a cohesive, professional look and feel. Your feed is recognizable at a glance.
  • 12
    Strong, Opinionated Hooks
    Your captions and video intros grab attention immediately. The first line makes someone stop scrolling.
  • 13
    Proof of Results
    You regularly share case studies, testimonials, before-and-afters, or client wins. Social proof is visible on your feed.
  • 14
    Founder-Led Content
    If you're a founder-led brand, you personally show up in your content to build trust and human connection.
Section 03

Engagement & Community

How actively you're building relationships with your audience.

  • 15
    Engaging Captions
    Your captions are written to encourage saves, shares, and comments — not just passive scrolling.
  • 16
    Strategic Use of Stories
    You use Stories for behind-the-scenes content, Q&As, and direct audience engagement — not just reposts.
  • 17
    Interactive Story Features
    You regularly use polls, quizzes, and question stickers to interact with your audience and gather insights.
  • 18
    Timely DM Responses
    You have a system for responding to DMs within 24 hours. DMs are treated as a sales channel, not an inbox.
  • 19
    Comment Replies
    You reply to comments on your posts to foster conversation and signal to the algorithm that your content is worth showing.
Section 04

Conversion & Business Impact

Whether your Instagram is actually driving real business results.

  • 20
    Clear Calls to Action
    Every post has a clear CTA that tells the viewer exactly what to do next. "Link in bio" alone doesn't count.
  • 21
    Funnel Stage Alignment
    Your content mix is aligned with your audience's level of awareness — you're not just posting BOFU content to cold audiences.
  • 22
    Highlights as Sales Tools
    Your Story Highlights are organized into strategic folders that act as a sales resource (e.g., "About," "Services," "Results," "FAQ").
  • 23
    Clear Path to Purchase
    It's incredibly easy for a follower to understand how to buy from you or book a call. The journey is frictionless.
  • 24
    Conversion-Focused Formats
    You use formats designed to convert — carousels that teach, Reels that demonstrate, and posts that build desire.
  • 25
    Tracking & Measurement
    You know which posts are driving profile visits, link clicks, and leads. You make decisions based on data, not gut.
  • 26
    Offer-Aware Content
    Your content speaks directly to the specific problems your paid offers solve. Followers know what you sell.
  • 27
    Lead Nurturing System
    You have a system (like an email list) to nurture leads who aren't ready to buy immediately. Followers don't just disappear.
