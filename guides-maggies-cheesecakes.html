Chapter 01

Why Most Food Businesses
Stay Invisible on Instagram

Five gaps that keep talented food businesses from converting followers into paying customers — and how to close them.

Gap 01 — Universal
No Defined Ideal Viewer Profile (IVP)
Why It Matters
Without a clear IVP, content tries to speak to everyone — and ends up resonating with no one. A corporate event planner, a bride planning a wedding dessert table, and a gift-giver shopping for a unique birthday present all need different messages, different hooks, and different CTAs.
The Fix
Define 2–3 specific IVPs for Maggie's: the corporate catering buyer, the event host, and the gifting customer. Build content pillars, hooks, and CTAs for each. When the right person sees the right message, they don't scroll — they order.
Gap 02 — Universal
Posting Without a Funnel
Why It Matters
Most food businesses post beautiful product photos with no strategic intent. Every piece of content should serve a purpose: building awareness, nurturing interest, or driving a specific action. Without funnel alignment, you're creating content that looks great but converts nothing.
The Fix
Map every post to a funnel stage. TOFU content builds awareness and reach (process videos, behind-the-scenes). MOFU content builds trust and consideration (testimonials, catering proof). BOFU content drives orders (limited availability, direct CTAs, DM triggers).
Gap 03 — Universal
No Social SEO Strategy
Why It Matters
Instagram is a search engine. People are actively searching for "mini cheesecake catering," "dessert table ideas," and "corporate event desserts" right now. If your profile, bio, captions, and alt text aren't optimized for these searches, you're invisible to the people who are ready to order.
The Fix
Build a keyword bank specific to Maggie's three revenue streams (catering, events, gifting). Embed keywords naturally in the bio, captions, and content. Optimize every reel with a keyword-rich hook in the first line of the caption — that's what Instagram indexes.
Gap 04 — Food & Bev Specific
The Product Is the Content — But Nobody's Showing the Process
Why It Matters
For food businesses, the most powerful content isn't the finished product — it's the process. Watching a cheesecake come together, seeing the care that goes into each mini, understanding the craft behind the product — this is what builds desire, trust, and emotional connection before a single order is placed.
The Fix
Build a consistent "behind the bake" content system. Film the pour, the crust press, the flavor development, the packaging. These videos are inherently watchable, highly shareable, and they do the selling without ever feeling like a sales pitch. The product sells itself when people see how it's made.
Gap 05 — Food & Bev Specific
No Corporate or Event Catering Content System
Why It Matters
Maggie's highest-value orders come from corporate catering and events — but most of the content that wins on Instagram is consumer-facing. The corporate buyer is watching Instagram too, but they need to see social proof that Maggie's can handle volume, presentation, and logistics at scale. That content doesn't exist yet.
The Fix
Create a dedicated content pillar for corporate and event catering. Show large-format orders being prepared and delivered. Feature setup photos, branded packaging, and happy client moments. This content positions Maggie's as a professional catering operation — not just a home baker — and speaks directly to the corporate buyer's decision criteria.

Lead With What You Do, Then Who It's For

The Behind-the-Bake Process Reel
TOFU Process Video
✦ Creative Format — Satisfying Process Content
"Watch me make 200 mini cheesecakes for a corporate event from start to finish."
Why It Works
Process content is the most watched and saved category in food. It builds desire, demonstrates craft, and creates trust before any sales message is needed.
Execution
Film the full process in one session — crust, filling, pour, chill, unmold, plate. Edit to 30–60 seconds with satisfying transitions. Add text overlays with the flavor name and event type.
Sammy Jones Script Framework
1
Hook (0–3s)
Open on the most visually satisfying moment — the pour, the unmold, or the finished tray. Text overlay: "200 mini cheesecakes for a corporate event. Here's how it's done."
2
Build (3–25s)
Show each stage of the process in sequence — crust, filling, bake, chill, unmold, garnish, package. Keep cuts tight and satisfying. Add text overlays naming each step.
3
Value (25–45s)
Show the finished product — the full tray, the setup at the event, the branded packaging. Let the visual quality do the selling.
4
CTA (45–60s)
End with: "Planning an event? DM CATERING for our corporate menu and pricing." Close on the Maggie's logo or a branded packaging shot.

Chapter 05

The CTA System

A call-to-action isn't just "link in bio." Every piece of content needs a specific, intentional next step that matches where the viewer is in their decision process.

"Every post should move someone one step closer to ordering — not just closer to following."
🎯
Match the CTA to the funnel stage. A TOFU post asking for a booking is too aggressive. A BOFU post asking for a follow is a missed conversion.
🔑
Use keyword triggers. "Comment MENU" or "DM CATERING" are trackable, automatable, and far more specific than "link in bio."
📍
One destination per post. Don't give people three options — they'll choose none. Pick the most important next step and make it the only one.
TOFU — Awareness
The Save Trigger
For educational or planning content. Encourages saves, which signals to the algorithm that the content is valuable and boosts distribution.
Example for Maggie's
"Save this for your next event planning session — you'll want this flavor guide when you're building your dessert table."
TOFU — Awareness
The Comment Trigger
Drives comments (boosting algorithmic reach) while capturing warm leads via automation. The comment keyword triggers an automated DM with your menu or guide.
Example for Maggie's
"Comment MENU below and I'll send you our full catering menu and pricing directly to your DMs — no forms, no waiting."
MOFU — Consideration
The DM Opener
Invites a direct conversation. Best for custom orders, catering inquiries, or any situation where the order requires a conversation before a price can be given.
Example for Maggie's
"Planning a corporate event? DM CATERING — tell me your guest count and date and I'll build a custom quote within 24 hours."
MOFU — Consideration
The Follow Prompt
Used strategically on high-reach content to convert new viewers into followers. Only use when the content is genuinely valuable enough to earn a follow.
Example for Maggie's
"Follow for weekly flavor drops, behind-the-bake content, and first access to new catering packages."
BOFU — Conversion
The Direct Order CTA
For ready-to-buy viewers. Removes friction by giving a single, clear next step to place an order. Best paired with urgency or scarcity language.
Example for Maggie's
"Ready to order? Click the link in bio — or DM BOOK to claim one of our remaining [Month] catering spots before they're gone."
BOFU — Conversion
The Urgency Close
Combines scarcity with a direct action. Most effective when the scarcity is genuine — limited monthly slots, seasonal flavors, or a specific event deadline.
Example for Maggie's
"Only 3 catering slots left for December. DM BOOK now — we fill up fast during the holiday season and can't take late additions."
The One Link Rule
Your link in bio should route different visitors to the right destination — not send everyone to your homepage.

Order Now
Direct link to your online order form or shop. For retail and gift orders.
02
Catering Inquiry
A simple form: event date, guest count, flavor preferences, contact info. Qualifies leads before you spend time on a quote.
03
Gift Boxes
A dedicated page for gifting options — individual boxes, custom sets, corporate gifting programs.
