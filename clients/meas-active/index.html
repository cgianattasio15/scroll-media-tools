Strategic Direction
Account narrative and Instagram's role for this client

MEAS Active is a women's activewear brand founded by Erin Roddy, a competitive runner and entrepreneur based in Cincinnati. The brand name "MEAS" derives from the Cambodian word for gold — a nod to Erin's philanthropic mission of empowering women globally. MEAS donates a portion of every sale to women's causes, making the brand's identity inseparable from its mission.

The brand competes in a crowded activewear market dominated by Lululemon, Fabletics, and viral DTC brands. MEAS's differentiation is threefold: a founder-led story no corporate brand can replicate, performance-first design from a founder who actually competes, and a philanthropic mission that gives the IVP a reason to choose MEAS over a brand she already knows.

Instagram's three-part job: (1) grow new audience and build brand identity around the MEAS mission — Erin's story, the Cambodia origin, the women's empowerment angle — among women who value brands that stand for something; (2) nurture the community and convert followers into email subscribers through mission-driven content and product education; (3) drive e-commerce shop traffic and email list sign-ups through specific product CTAs and wholesale partnership visibility.

At Lift stage with 10,200 followers, MEAS has strong CTR (13–14%) and PCR (8–16%) signals. The strategic priority is growing the TOFU audience through community and mission content while maintaining the BOFU engine that drives shop traffic and email captures.

IVP Profile
Ideal Viewer / Prospect — who we're talking to and what they need
Demographics
  • Women, ages 25–40, national (US), Cincinnati-rooted community
  • Active lifestyle — running, fitness, yoga, or general movement
  • Household income $60K–$130K+
  • Values-driven consumer — supports women-owned, mission-led brands
  • Active on Instagram; follows fitness, wellness, activewear content
Psychographics
  • Wants activewear that performs AND looks elevated off the mat
  • Values brands with a real story — not just a product
  • Motivated by community and shared identity (not just aesthetics)
  • Skeptical of greenwashing and corporate "mission" marketing — wants authenticity
  • Proud to support brands that give back to causes she believes in
Core Pain Points
  • Brand loyalty to Lululemon — needs a compelling reason to switch or add MEAS
  • Sizing and fit anxiety — activewear fit is personal and high-stakes
  • Price justification — $65+ for leggings requires proof of quality and value
  • Discovery gap — doesn't know MEAS exists or what makes it different
Dream Outcome

Tangible: A complete activewear wardrobe she trusts — pieces that perform at every intensity level, look elevated enough for errands and coffee dates, and hold up wash after wash. A brand she recommends without hesitation.

Emotional: Feels like MEAS was built specifically for how she lives. Wears it and feels confident without trying too hard. Proud to represent a brand whose mission she believes in. Part of a community of women who move and show up for themselves.

Counter-Positioning
vs. Lululemon / Fabletics

Founder-led brand with a real mission vs. corporation selling an aspirational lifestyle. MEAS name origin story (Cambodia, gold) is a differentiator no $5B brand can replicate. Community-first positioning vs. scale-first marketing.

vs. Viral DTC (Halara, Girlfriend Collective)

Mission and story-driven, not trend-cycle dependent. Performance-first design from a founder who actually runs competitively. Authentic Cincinnati roots create a local loyalty layer national brands can't fake.

vs. Adjacent Lifestyle Brands

MEAS leads with identity and mission, not just elevated aesthetics. Founder vulnerability and personal story create deeper emotional connection. Philanthropic giving to women's causes is a real differentiator.

MEAS Advantage

Founded on a true story of women's empowerment + performance-first design by a female athlete + philanthropic giving + Cincinnati roots with national ambition + founder vulnerability no competitor can replicate.

Cross-Pillar Priorities
Erin Roddy's founder story and personal athletic journey should surface across ALL three pillars. The MEAS origin story (Cambodia, meaning of gold) is a recurring content asset — not a one-time post. Wholesale positioning (B2B buyer angle) lives within Street to Studio Looks. Jan 2026 data: CTR (14.1%) and PCR (16.1%) are strong — conversion intent exists, content must direct it.
