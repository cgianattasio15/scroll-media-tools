Strategic Direction
Account narrative and Instagram's role for this client

DEFINE Oakley is a boutique fitness studio in Cincinnati's Oakley Square neighborhood, offering beat-based cycle (Rev), barre (Body), and trampoline HIIT (Bounce) classes. The studio's signature 30:30 format — 30 minutes of Rev followed by 30 minutes of Body — is its primary differentiator: two full workouts in one 60-minute session, built for busy, time-conscious women who refuse to choose between cardio and toning.

The studio's second major differentiator is on-site childcare, which removes the single biggest barrier for its core audience: moms and expecting women who want to prioritize fitness but can't leave their kids. No other boutique studio in Cincinnati combines this level of format efficiency with childcare access.

01
Drive new class bookings by showcasing the energy, community, and results of the studio experience
02
Retain existing members by reinforcing community identity and celebrating studio culture
03
Reduce churn by consistently demonstrating the value of the premium membership
Content Strategy Map
TOFU / MOFU / BOFU — goals, descriptions, KPIs, and content formats
Goal
Top of Funnel 50%
Drive New Audience Discovery & Brand Awareness
Build awareness among Cincinnati's busy women, moms, and fitness-curious audience. Position Define Oakley as the most efficient, community-driven boutique fitness studio in the city. Introduce the 30:30 format, the Bounce class, and the childcare differentiator to cold audiences.
Middle of Funnel 30%
Build Trust, Educate, & Deepen Community Connection
Nurture the audience evaluating whether to book their first class or upgrade their membership. Demonstrate the value of the 30:30 format through education and social proof. Address objections around cost, intimidation, and childcare logistics.
Bottom of Funnel 20%
Drive Class Bookings & New Member Sign-Ups
Convert engaged followers and profile visitors into paying members. Drive clicks to the Define Oakley booking page through class-specific content, first-timer offers, and direct booking CTAs. Reduce friction by naming the specific class, time, and booking destination.

Content Pillars
The 4 core themes every piece of content should align to
Pillar 01
Efficiency & Format Education
Education · Authority · Curiosity
Sell the Rev + Body 30:30 combo and the full class format lineup (Rev, Body, Bounce). Explain why the format works, who it's built for, and why it outperforms separate cardio and toning sessions. Position Define as the smart choice for busy women who refuse to compromise on results.
TOFU — Drive New Audience Discovery
Pillar 02
Community & Culture
Connection · Trust · Social Proof
Show the human side of Define. Behind-the-scenes class energy, member spotlights, instructor personalities, milestone celebrations, and the moments that make Define feel like home. Retention content that keeps existing members emotionally invested and shows prospects what they're missing.
MOFU — Build Trust & Deepen Connection
Pillar 03
Mom-Friendly Positioning
Connection · Trust · Authority
Emphasize childcare access, flexible scheduling, and the fact that Define is built for women who want to prioritize themselves without sacrificing their family. Define's most powerful competitive differentiator — it directly removes the #1 barrier for the core IVP. Feature real mom members and childcare in action.
BOFU — Drive Class Bookings
Pillar 04
Authority & Wellness Perspective
Education · Authority · Trust
Build trust through education. Share insights on training principles, recovery, consistency, the psychology of sustainable fitness, and the science behind beat-based training. Position Define's instructors as credible experts, not just class leaders. Earns long-term trust and reduces cost-justification objections.
TOFU — Drive New Audience Discovery
IVP Profile
Ideal Viewer / Prospect — who we're talking to and what they need
Demographics
  • Women, ages 25–45
  • Household income $75K–$150K+
  • Cincinnati metro area (Oakley, Hyde Park, Mariemont, Indian Hill)
  • Moms, expecting women, and career-driven professionals
  • Boutique fitness buyers — already spending on wellness
Psychographics
  • Time-conscious — efficiency is non-negotiable
  • Community-driven — wants belonging, not just a workout
  • Results-oriented — expects visible progress
  • Aesthetics-aware — cares about the environment and experience
  • Guilt-prone — struggles to prioritize herself without justification
Core Pain Points
  • Time scarcity — can't spend 2 hours at a gym
  • Childcare logistics — no studio = no workout
  • Intimidation — fears being the "new person"
  • Cost justification — boutique pricing needs proof of value
Dream Outcome

Tangible: Shows up 3–4x/week consistently. Completes the 30:30 without modifying. Feels physically stronger and more energized. Has a standing class time that's non-negotiable in her schedule.

Emotional: Feels like she's finally carved out time for herself without guilt. Leaves every class feeling accomplished. Feels part of a community that knows her name.

Counter-Positioning
vs. Big-Box Gyms

Expert-led, beat-based classes vs. machines and mirrors. Community culture — you know instructors by name. 30:30 delivers more in 60 min than a 2-hour gym session.

vs. Other Boutique Studios

Only studio in Cincinnati offering Rev + Body 30:30 in one session. Only Bounce (trampoline HIIT) studio in Cincinnati. On-site childcare — no other boutique studio offers this.

vs. At-Home Fitness

Accountability and community that at-home fitness can't replicate. Instructor energy and live music create an experience. Childcare on-site removes the "I can't leave the kids" excuse entirely.
