Realistic 5-Month Value Generated

For a community-driven, trust-based business like Up and Running, the path from a viewer seeing a Reel to walking into your store or buying online is not a straight line — but it is measurable. We track what we call High-Intent Actions (HIAs): the moments when someone moves from passive viewer to active consideration. The most trackable HIA is a link tap — someone who saw your content, visited your profile, and clicked through to your website. That person is a warm lead. They're one good experience away from becoming a customer.

"For a business like yours, sales are built on trust, not clicks. Our job is to build that trust at scale — turning Instagram from a marketing channel into a core piece of your sales infrastructure."

The Number You Cannot See — But Is Very Real

The tracked numbers above are the floor, not the ceiling. They only count people who clicked a link. They don't count the person who saw the Frozen Feet Challenge Reel and mentioned it to a friend. They don't count the run club member who saw your community content and told their group about you. For a local business with strong community roots, the in-store influence of social media is often 3–5x the tracked digital impact. That puts the realistic 5-month value of this partnership at $2,500–$5,000+ — against a $8,131 investment that also built a long-term brand asset that compounds over time.
