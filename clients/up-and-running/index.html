Strategic Direction
Account narrative and Instagram's role for this client

Up and Running is a Dayton, Ohio-based specialty running and fitness retail store with two locations (Dayton + Troy). The brand's core differentiator is the expert shoe fitting process — staff trained in gait mechanics, arch type, and foot biomechanics who recommend the right shoe regardless of brand. This is the opposite of the self-service shoe wall at Dick's or the guesswork of buying online.

The business serves a wide mover audience — competitive runners, casual walkers, on-your-feet workers, and returning movers — but the unifying thread is that every customer benefits from expert guidance. The fitting process is the product. The store's community programming (run groups, walk groups, events) creates a loyalty layer that no big-box retailer can replicate.

Instagram's three-part job: (1) build brand awareness and grow new audience among every type of mover in the Dayton/Ohio market — position Up and Running as the go-to movement resource, not just a shoe store; (2) build trust through expertise — demonstrate the fitting process, share gear guides, injury prevention content, and community moments that make the brand feel like a trusted local partner; (3) drive e-commerce shop traffic and in-store fit booking appointments through specific product CTAs and direct booking links.

At Spark stage with ~2,307 followers, this is an early-stage account focused on establishing brand identity and building the content engine. Feb 2026 showed strong momentum (1,772 avg reach/day, 32 shares, 8.3 score). The strategic priority is growing the local audience while establishing the expert fitting process as the primary brand differentiator through consistent educational content.

IVP Profile
Ideal Viewer / Prospect — who we're talking to and what they need
Demographics
  • Women 28–55, Dayton/Troy/Ohio market (primary)
  • Active lifestyle — running, walking, fitness, or on-your-feet work
  • Household income $50K–$120K
  • Community-oriented — values local businesses over big-box retail
  • Follows local fitness, wellness, and community content on Instagram
Psychographics
  • Wants to feel supported in her movement journey — not just sold a shoe
  • Values expert guidance over self-service — overwhelmed by options without help
  • Loyal to local businesses that invest in the community
  • Motivated by community events, run groups, and shared experiences
  • Trusts word-of-mouth and staff expertise over brand marketing
Core Pain Points
  • Wrong shoe purchases — has bought shoes online or at big-box that didn't work, causing discomfort or injury
  • Overwhelmed by options — too many brands, too many models, no trusted guide
  • Doesn't know the fitting process exists — awareness gap is the #1 TOFU challenge
  • Price justification — specialty running shoes cost more; needs to understand why the fitting is worth it
Dream Outcome

Tangible: A shoe that fits perfectly, recommended by someone who actually understands her foot, gait, and activity level. Shoes she wears confidently without pain, blisters, or second-guessing. A go-to store she recommends to every person in her running group.

Emotional: Feels like she has a trusted expert in her corner. Part of a local movement community that supports and celebrates every level of mover. Proud to support a local business that genuinely cares about her experience.

Counter-Positioning
vs. Big-Box Retail (Dick's, Academy)

Expert fitting process vs. self-service shoe walls with no guidance. Staff trained in gait mechanics, arch type, and foot biomechanics. Brand-agnostic recommendations — the right shoe for you, not the shoe on sale.

vs. DTC Brands (Hoka, Brooks, On Running online)

In-person fitting eliminates sizing guesswork and return cycles. Curates across all brands — not locked into one brand's catalog. Local community layer no DTC brand can replicate.

vs. Amazon / Online Shopping

Eliminates the costly order-return-reorder cycle. Expert guidance available both in-store and through content. Community programming creates ongoing relationship, not a one-time transaction.

Up and Running Advantage

Two Dayton-area locations + community-first operating model + expert fitting as the core product + active programming (run groups, walk groups, events) + staff who are movers themselves.
