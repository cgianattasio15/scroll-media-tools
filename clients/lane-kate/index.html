Strategic Direction
Account narrative and Instagram's role for this client

Lane & Kate is a Cincinnati fine jewelry boutique specializing in engagement rings, custom designs, and elevated everyday jewelry. Their core differentiator is an education-first, pressure-free consultation experience — the antithesis of the big-box jewelry store model (Jared, Kay, Zales) where commission-driven sales staff push inventory over fit.

The engagement ring category is one of the highest-consideration purchases a consumer makes. The buyer researches extensively online before ever walking into a store. Instagram's role for Lane & Kate is to intercept that research phase — building familiarity, educating on the 4Cs and custom design process, and positioning L&K as the trusted local expert before the consultation is even booked.

Instagram's three-part job: (1) boost local discoverability among Cincinnati couples beginning their engagement ring search; (2) build purchase confidence through education — diamond guides, metal comparisons, custom process explainers — that makes the IVP feel informed and less overwhelmed; (3) drive qualified consultation bookings from followers who already trust L&K's expertise before they walk in the door.

At Lift stage with 7,143 followers, the account has strong CTR and PCR signals — the conversion intent is there. The strategic priority is building a larger warm audience through consistent educational and aspirational content while maintaining the BOFU engine that drives consultation bookings.

IVP Profile
Ideal Viewer / Prospect — who we're talking to and what they need
Demographics
  • Women & men, ages 25–38, Cincinnati metro area
  • Household income $75K–$200K+
  • Engaged or pre-engagement research phase
  • College-educated, career-focused, values-driven
  • Active Instagram users; follows bridal, jewelry, lifestyle content
Psychographics
  • Wants a ring that tells their unique love story — not a generic box-store option
  • Values expert guidance without sales pressure
  • Researches extensively before making high-stakes purchases
  • Appreciates craftsmanship and wants to understand what they're paying for
  • Prefers local boutique experience over impersonal e-commerce
Core Pain Points
  • Overwhelm — 4Cs, settings, metals, lab vs. natural — too many decisions
  • Fear of wrong choice — once-in-a-lifetime purchase, high emotional stakes
  • Budget anxiety — unsure how much to spend or where the money goes
  • Time constraints — wants to research online before committing to in-person visits
Dream Outcome

Tangible: An engagement ring she absolutely loves and feels confident in. Understands what she paid for and why it was worth it. Guided through the process by a trusted expert — not pressured.

Emotional: Confident in the choice (no regrets). The purchase feels intentional and personal. The boutique experience felt special — not transactional. Trusts the jeweler they worked with long after the sale.

Counter-Positioning
vs. Jared / Kay / Zales

Personalized service vs. commission-driven pressure. Curated selection vs. overwhelming inventory. Expert guidance vs. upselling. Local boutique vs. corporate chain.

vs. Blue Nile / James Allen

Personal guidance vs. faceless e-commerce. Local boutique experience vs. algorithm-driven browsing. Custom design support vs. DIY overwhelm. You can touch and try before you buy.

L&K Advantage

Education-first approach + curated selection + personalized service + local Cincinnati boutique. The ring tells your love story — not a catalog number.
