**Subject:** Your profile is losing you clients
**Preview:** Here's a 5-point fix.

Hey {{ subscriber.first_name | default: "there" }},

Most service businesses have the same problem:

They're posting consistently. Getting some engagement. But no clients are coming through.

9 times out of 10 — it's not the content. **It's the profile.**

Your Instagram profile is your new homepage. A stranger lands there and decides in 3 seconds whether to trust you or bounce. If it's not set up to convert, your content doesn't matter.

Here's a quick 5-point audit:

**1. Bio** — Before you write a single word, answer two questions:
- **What's your primary niche?** (Be specific. "Social media marketing" is a category. "Social media for med spas" is a niche.)
- **What's your differentiator?** What do you do that the other 1,000 people in your niche don't? That's what earns market share.

Your bio should make both answers obvious in one sentence.
- ❌ "Interior designer | Cincinnati, OH"
- ✅ "Helping Cincinnati homeowners design spaces they love coming home to — without the renovation stress"

**2. Link in Bio** — One clear CTA. Not a Linktree with 10 options.
Give them one door — a booking page, a lead magnet, or a contact form.

**3. Pinned Posts** — Think of these as a mini-funnel:
- Pin 1: **Founder Story** → builds trust
- Pin 2: **Business Intro + CTA** → drives action (must match your link in bio)
- Pin 3: **Best-performing post** → reinforces credibility

**4. Highlights** — Max 5–7, branded covers, no expired content.
For service businesses: About · Results · Services · Reviews · Contact

**5. Grid** — Zoom out and look at your last 9 posts.
Does it look like a premium brand? If not, that's what people are seeing first.

Fix these five things before posting another piece of content.

In two days, I'll show you why your best content might be getting engagement but still failing to book clients — and it has nothing to do with the content itself.
