01 — Stage Classification

Your targets are built around your stage,
not just your follower count.

We use a 4-stage maturity model to classify every account. Follower count is the starting point, but your actual stage is determined by your engagement quality, content system maturity, and how your key metrics compare to what we'd expect at each level.

Stage 01
Spark
0 – 2,500 followers
Building the foundation. Testing what resonates. Establishing a consistent content system.
Stage 02
Lift
2,500 – 10K followers
Scaling what works. Building conversion paths. Growing a qualified audience.
Stage 03
Rise
10K – 100K followers
Optimizing at scale. Building brand authority. Deepening audience trust.
Stage 04
Thrive
100K+ followers
Maximizing brand equity. Ecosystem building. Compounding long-term authority.
Why This Matters

Stage assignment is a protection mechanism — not just a label.

We can't change your account's performance overnight. If we assigned you to the wrong stage, we'd be measuring you against the wrong targets from day one — either setting expectations you can't realistically reach yet, or setting the bar so low the data tells you nothing. Your stage is validated against your actual engagement signals and reviewed every 60–90 days as your performance evolves.

02 — Benchmark Methodology

Three inputs. One honest benchmark.

The specific high and low targets for each metric were developed using a deliberate, multi-input process — not guesswork, and not conservative defaults designed to make everything look good.

1
Industry Benchmark Research

We analyzed performance data across the service-based businesses we work with — med spas, coaching, fitness, real estate, home services — to understand what normal, strong, and exceptional performance looks like at each stage. These aren't averages from a generic social media blog. They reflect what actually happens inside accounts in the niches we operate in.

2
Instagram Algorithm Priority Signals

The metrics we track — especially the ones weighted most heavily — reflect what Instagram demonstrably rewards. Saves and shares tell the algorithm the content is worth distributing further. Retention rate tells us whether content is holding attention long enough to matter. These choices are grounded in how the platform actually works right now, not how it worked two years ago.

3
Semi-Aggressive Calibration — By Design

Targets are intentionally set above what's easy to achieve. A benchmark you hit effortlessly every month tells you nothing useful. Our targets are built to push performance forward — so when you score "On Track," you know you're genuinely building momentum. When something shows as "Watch," it's a real signal worth addressing, not a technicality.

03 — Metric Weighting

Not all metrics are equal.
Here's how we prioritize them.

We track 12 metrics across the full funnel, but they don't impact your score equally. We use a weighted tier system because volume metrics and intent metrics tell completely different stories. A post with 10,000 views and zero saves is not the same as a post with 2,000 views and 80 saves.

Tier Metrics Included Why We Weight These This Way
Tier 1 Highest Weight Saves, Shares, Retention %, Click-Through Rate, Profile Conversion Rate High-intent actions. A save means someone found the content valuable enough to return to. A share means they trusted it enough to put their name on it. These are buying signals — not vanity metrics. Instagram also uses these signals to determine how widely to distribute content.
Tier 2 Standard Weight Avg. Reach/Day, New Followers, Total Views, Profile Visits, Link Taps Important growth indicators, but partially algorithm-dependent. Strong volume without strong Tier 1 signals usually means the content is reaching people — but not moving them. We track these closely, but they don't carry the same strategic weight.
Tier 3 Supporting Weight Comments, Avg. Watch Time Valuable supporting signals, but highly variable depending on content type and topic. A tutorial drives more comments than a brand post. A 30-second Reel has a different watch time benchmark than a 90-second one. We track them — they just don't carry the same weight as saves or shares.
04 — Your Monthly Score

What the score actually means.

Each month, every metric is evaluated against your stage-specific target range and assigned one of three statuses. Those statuses feed into your weighted monthly score — out of 10.

Exceeding
Above the high end of your target range. Strong signal — keep doing what's working and double down on the formats driving it.
On Track
Within the target range. Healthy, compounding performance. Not easy to achieve — that's the point. This is where you want to be.
Watch
Below the target floor. A real signal that requires a strategic response — not a judgment. Every Watch area comes with a clear action plan.
Trend matters too. The score also factors in month-over-month trajectory — consistent improvement is rewarded even when you haven't hit every target yet. A client moving in the right direction across most metrics will score better than one who is flat, even if the flat client's raw numbers are higher.
The Bottom Line

Honest benchmarks. Clear signals. No guesswork.

Your targets exist to give you a stage-appropriate benchmark — not to grade you against accounts twice your size, and not to make everything look good when it isn't. Every range was built to reflect what healthy, compounding growth actually looks like at your stage. When we flag something as a Watch area, it's because the data says so — and we'll always pair it with a clear action plan.
