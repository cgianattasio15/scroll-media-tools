The three operating principles
  1. Channel sequencing is the moat. A-tier prospects feel four touches: an IG follow + engagement layer, a LinkedIn connection note, a cold email, a chase.scrolls DM. The compound is what closes them.
  2. Attention before pitch. No pitch goes out before a warm-up. Email leans on cold-email-specific warm-up (subject, founder story). IG leans on 5-day engagement. LinkedIn leans on the connection note + 3-7 day pause before DM 1.
  3. Update the sheet within 60 seconds of the action. Same rule as the team. The system breaks the moment your worklist drifts from reality.

H. Troubleshooting (7 common scenarios)

Scenario 1: Prospect asks pricing in Email 1 reply

Read: High-intent prospect skipping the funnel. Treat as warm but don't lead with the price tag.

Hey [Name],

Real quick on pricing. We work in two tiers, Essential and Growth, starting at $2,975/mo with a $1,917 strategy build up front. Three-month minimum on the retainer, then month-to-month.

But pricing follows fit. Want to book a 20-min discovery so I can hear what you're trying to do? If we're a fit, I'll send a proposal. If we're not, you'll have a clearer read on what to look for.

https://calendly.com/scrollmedia/discovery

Cheers, Chase.

Scenario 2: Prospect goes hostile

Read: Done. Respect immediately. One line, no defensiveness.

All good, [Name]. Best of luck with [Business Name].

Cheers.

Remove from active sequence. Mark Closed-Lost with reason "Hostile / unsubscribe."

Scenario 3: Prospect ghosts after the Manual share

Read: Either the Manual didn't land, the niche fit is off, or they're busy. Don't take it personally.

  1. Don't send a third follow-up after the audit offer goes unanswered for 7 days.
  2. Wait 14 days from the audit offer date.
  3. Fire Email 5 / DM 4 if their Engagement Signals show any opens / story watches / profile visits.
  4. If Email 5 also goes unanswered for 14 days, mark Closed-Lost with reason "Ghosted post-Manual."
  5. Add to a 6-month re-engagement reminder.

Scenario 4: Prospect responds warmly but won't switch from IG/LinkedIn to email/call

Read: They're vetting you. Don't force the channel switch. Build the relationship until they're ready.

  1. Keep the conversation on the channel they prefer.
  2. Continue sharing value (specific tactical insights, the Manual if not yet shared, niche peer observations).
  3. Track every reply in Engagement Signals.
  4. After 3-4 substantive exchanges, ask once: "Hey, want to jump on a 15-min call this week? Easier to walk through what I'm seeing than type it." Send the Calendly link.
  5. If they say no but stay engaged, keep building. Some prospects close 3-6 months after first touch.

Scenario 5: Prospect says "send me more info" or "send a deck"

Read: They want the homework before the call.

  1. Don't send a deck cold. The deck without context is a generic agency pitch.
  2. Respond: "Happy to send something more specific to [Business Name]. The fastest path is a 15-min call where I can hear what you're solving for, then I'll send a tailored proposal after. Want to grab time? https://calendly.com/scrollmedia/discovery"
  3. If they push back, send the Manual link as the first step.
  4. If they still won't book, send a one-pager with three white-labeled proof points and the Calendly link.

Scenario 6: Multi-channel touches collide and the prospect calls it out

Read: Good. They're noticing the saturation. That's the compounding signal working.

  1. Own it. Don't pretend the channels are unconnected.
  2. Respond: "Ha, yeah, that was me. We work multi-channel for a few reasons but mostly because email lands one way, LinkedIn another. Glad it caught your attention here. [continue the substantive conversation]."
  3. Update Engagement Signals with note: Multi-channel acknowledgment [date].
  4. Treat as a warm signal.

Scenario 7: Day breaks · client crisis, sick day, travel

Read: Don't panic. The system is consistency over time, not hero days.

  1. Priority order if the day collapses: Email > LinkedIn > IG > content > audits.
  2. Fire as many cold emails as you can.
  3. Skip the LinkedIn / IG day-of motion if needed. The sequences are forgiving on 1-2 day slips.
  4. Do NOT batch-make-up the next day (don't send 50 emails Wednesday to compensate for Tuesday's 0). Lumpy sending is a deliverability tell.
  5. Update Next Touch Date for any prospect whose action you skipped.
  6. Take Friday off if you need it. Family time discipline still applies.
I. Sheet hygiene rules (binding)
  • Next Touch Date is sacred. Update it within 60 seconds of every touch. If it drifts, your daily worklist filter goes wrong and prospects fall through.
  • Engagement Signals gets every meaningful event. Opens, clicks, story reacts, comment replies, DM replies, profile views. The audit trail.
  • Funnel Stage updates immediately on stage transitions. Don't wait for end-of-week.
  • Closed-Lost gets a Closed Reason. Always. "Ghosted post-Manual," "Pricing shock," "Hostile / unsubscribe," "Wrong fit," "Closed-Won by competitor," etc.
  • Owner column K stays Chase for A/B-tier. Single-owner model. Don't reassign mid-sequence.

Section 08

LinkedIn posting strategy

When you send a cold LinkedIn DM, the prospect checks the profile before deciding to reply. If the feed posts signal founder-led credibility + Cincinnati relevance + IG depth inside one scroll, the DM converts at a materially higher rate.

No engagement layer (May 11 decision). No daily commenting ritual on other founders' posts. Founders don't post much on LinkedIn themselves. The compounding signal LinkedIn actually needs is "the cold DM lands on a profile with credible recent posts." That's the post-publishing job. LinkedIn motion = feed posts (3-5/week) + DMs (25/day Tue/Wed/Thu). Nothing else.

The 4 LinkedIn acquisition pillars

Pillar 1 · 30%

Pillar 2 · 25%

Pillar 4 · 15%

Cadence and format

Cadence: 3-5 posts/week. Default 4/week. Posting days: Tue/Wed/Thu/Fri. Mon optional. Weekend off.

Format mix

Format% of mixWhy
LinkedIn-native text posts80-85%Algorithm rewards native text. 600-1,400 char sweet spot. Dominant format.
Text + single image10-15%1.3-1.5x reach over pure text. Use sparingly.

Character count targets

  • Sweet spot: 600-1,400 characters
  • Hook: hard cap at 150 characters (before "see more" cutoff)
  • Ceiling: 1,400 characters. Long-form rarely outperforms tight 900-char.

Format rules (binding)

  • No external links in post body. LinkedIn suppresses them. Link in first comment only if needed.
  • Short paragraphs. 1-2 sentences max per paragraph.
  • Hook in first 150 characters. Hard.
  • "DM me" CTAs > "link in bio." "DM me" outperforms.
  • Max 1 direct CTA per week. Other 3-4 posts pure value.
  • 3-5 hashtags max, bottom of post.
  • Never copy IG caption verbatim. Always reframe for LinkedIn's operator audience.

Stage progression rules

  • Funnel Stage moves forward, not backward. A prospect at DM2 Sent who engages warmly on the next email moves to Manual Shared once you share the Manual, not back to Engaged.

  • Manual Shared is the bridge stage. Most A-tier prospects who close go through this. If you skipped it and a prospect went straight from Engaged to Discovery Booked, ask whether you've left value on the table for the long tail.
