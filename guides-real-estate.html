Chapter 01

The Gaps

These aren't criticisms of your business. They're the patterns we see across almost every real estate agent on Instagram — and the reason consistent posting rarely turns into consistent leads.

Gap 01 — Universal
No Content Funnel
Why It Matters
Posting without a TOFU/MOFU/BOFU structure attracts attention but doesn't convert it. Reach without direction is just noise — and noise doesn't fill a pipeline.
The Fix
Map every post to a funnel stage — awareness, education, or action — so your content compounds instead of just accumulates. Every post should have a job. The deeper shift: stop building a listing feed and start building an agent brand. Listings are proof points — you are the product. Buyers and sellers don't hire a brokerage. They hire a person they trust. Your content is what builds that trust before the first conversation.
Gap 02 — Universal
No Social SEO
Why It Matters
Instagram is a search engine. Buyers and sellers search for agents in their market. Without keywords in your bio, captions, and audio, you're invisible to the exact people you want to reach.
The Fix
Build a keyword bank from your ICP's search behavior and embed it in every post. Your bio, captions, and Reel audio should all be optimized for how buyers and sellers actually search.
Gap 03 — Real Estate Specific
No Listing-Responsive Content System
Why It Matters
Most agents post about a new listing days after it goes live — or not at all. The window of peak buyer attention is the first 24–48 hours. Miss that window and you've left reach, engagement, and potential leads on the table every single time.
The Fix
Build a repeatable content system around every listing: a launch Reel within hours of going live, a behind-the-scenes Story series, and a sold announcement with results. Listings are your best content asset — treat them like it.
Gap 04 — Real Estate Specific
Posting Listings Instead of Building Authority
Why It Matters
A feed full of listing photos is a catalog, not a brand. Buyers and sellers don't choose an agent because they saw a house — they choose one because they trust the person. A catalog feed attracts browsers. An authority feed attracts clients.
The Fix
Lead with market insight, local expertise, and process transparency. Listings are proof points — not the main event. The agent is the brand. Here's the positioning shift that changes everything: your listing content tells people what you sell. Your agent content tells people who you are. High-intent buyers and sellers choose agents they trust — and trust is built through consistent, opinionated, expert-led content, not through property photos.
Gap 05 — Real Estate Specific
No IVP (Ideal Viewer Profile)
Why It Matters
Without a clear picture of who you're talking to — first-time buyer, move-up seller, investor, relocating professional — your content appeals to everyone and converts no one. Generic content gets generic results.
The Fix
Define your IVP before writing a single caption. Every post should feel like it was written for one specific person — the person most likely to become your next client. When you talk to everyone, you reach no one.

Where to Embed Keywords

👤
Name Field
The single most important SEO field on your profile. Instagram's search algorithm indexes this first. Include your name, role, and market.
"Jane Doe | Columbus OH Realtor"

Bio Copy
Weave 2–3 high-intent keywords naturally into your bio. Don't keyword-stuff — write for humans first, search second.

Caption First Line
Instagram indexes the first 125 characters of your caption. Lead with your keyword-rich hook — it's both SEO and the hook that stops the scroll.

🎙️
Reel Audio (Voiceover)
Instagram auto-transcribes Reel audio and uses it for search indexing. Say your keywords out loud in your Reels — especially in the first 5 seconds.
Open with: "If you're buying a home in Columbus this year, here's what you need to know..."
#️⃣

Use 5–8 targeted hashtags per post. Mix broad, niche, and hyper-local. Avoid generic hashtags with 50M+ posts — you'll never surface there.

Tag your location on every post and Reel. Neighborhood-level tags (Short North, Clintonville) outperform city-level tags for local discovery.
Tag the neighborhood, not just the city. "Short North, Columbus" beats "Columbus, Ohio" for local intent searches.

TOFU Reel
"New listing just hit the market — here's a full walkthrough before the open house this weekend."
Why It Works
Timeliness drives reach. A listing Reel posted within hours of going live catches the algorithm's recency window and signals to sellers: this agent moves fast.

60-sec walkthrough with voiceover on the 3 best features. Post within 24 hours of MLS activation. Follow with a Story poll: "Would you tour this one?"

Context & Conflict (0:03–0:20)
Set the scene: neighborhood, price point, standout feature. Establish why this listing matters to your IVP right now — buyer demand, limited inventory, location.
3
Turning Point (0:20–0:40)
Reveal the 3 features that make this property worth touring: the kitchen, the backyard, the location relative to [local landmark]. Show, don't just tell.

Resolution & CTA (0:40–0:60)

TOFU Carousel

Execution Note
5–7 slides. Slide 1: the headline shift. Slides 2–4: what it means for buyers, sellers, and the market. Slide 5: your take. Pull from MLS data monthly.
Script Framework

2

Turning Point (0:20–0:40)
Name the specific shift: rates moved, inventory dropped, a new development changed a neighborhood's trajectory. Be specific — vague market commentary is forgettable.
4
Resolution & CTA (0:40–0:60)
"Here's what this means for you if you're buying [X] or selling [Y] right now." Give one actionable takeaway per viewer type. End: "Save this — I'll update it monthly."

Chapter 05

The CTA System

CTAs are not afterthoughts. They are the conversion infrastructure that turns attention into measurable outcomes. Every post needs one — and it needs to be the right one for the funnel stage.

"Every piece of content must have a clear, intentional, on-strategy call to action. The CTA is what separates content that builds your audience from content that builds your business."
🎯
Funnel stage alignment is non-negotiable. A TOFU post with a "Book a call" CTA will kill your engagement. Match the ask to the relationship.
🔗
One link, one destination. Your link in bio must always point to a single, specific destination that matches your primary CTA. No Linktree. No homepage.
📣
The Follow CTA must earn its ask. Never use a generic "Follow for more." State the specific, tangible value the viewer gets in return.
✂️
Direct and specific beats clever. Name the destination. Name the outcome. "DM me 'BUYER' for my free first-time buyer checklist" outperforms "DM me for more info" every time.

CTA Types by Funnel Stage

TOFU — Awareness
Save CTA
Ask viewers to save the post for future reference. Saves are the highest-value engagement signal on Instagram — they tell the algorithm your content is worth keeping.
Example Copy
"Save this before your next home search — you'll want it when you're ready to make an offer."
TOFU — Awareness
Follow CTA
Earn the follow by stating the specific value. Generic "follow for more" is dead. Tell them exactly what they get by following you.
Example Copy
"Follow me for weekly Columbus market updates, neighborhood guides, and real talk about buying and selling."
MOFU — Education
Question CTA
Ask a specific question that invites your IVP to self-identify. Comments signal intent and surface warm leads without any direct selling.
Example Copy
"Are you a first-time buyer or a move-up seller? Drop it in the comments — I'll send you the guide that's most relevant to you."
MOFU — Education
Lead Magnet CTA
Offer a high-value, niche-specific resource in exchange for a DM or email. The best lead magnets solve a specific problem your IVP is already searching for.
Example Copy
"DM me 'GUIDE' and I'll send you my free Columbus First-Time Buyer Checklist — 12 steps before you make an offer."
BOFU — Conversion
Book / Inquire CTA
Direct invitation to start a conversation. Use only on BOFU content where the viewer has already seen enough to be in decision mode. Low friction, high intent.
Example Copy
"Thinking about selling this year? Tap the link in my bio to book a free 20-minute strategy call — no pressure, just a conversation."
BOFU — Conversion
DM Keyword CTA
Ask viewers to DM a specific keyword to receive a resource or start a conversation. The keyword triggers a warm conversation without a cold pitch — and filters for high-intent leads.
Example Copy
"DM me 'READY' and I'll send you my free Columbus Market Guide — everything you need to know before buying or selling in 2026."
The One Link, One Destination Rule
Your link in bio is your most valuable real estate on Instagram. Most agents waste it. Here's how to use it correctly.
✗
Don't use Linktree or multi-link pages. Every additional option you give a viewer reduces the probability they click any of them. Decision fatigue kills conversion.
✗
Don't link to your homepage. A homepage is a starting point, not a destination. Visitors who land on a homepage have to figure out what to do next — most won't.
✓
Link to one specific destination that matches your primary CTA for the month. If your CTA is "DM me for my market guide," your link should go to a page where they can download it directly.
✓
Change your link monthly. Align it with your primary business goal for the month — lead gen, bookings, or a specific listing. Update your bio copy to match.
✓
Track link taps. Link taps are the most important conversion metric on Instagram. They tell you how many people took action — not just how many people watched.
