Chapter 01

The Gaps

These aren't criticisms of your practice. They're the patterns we see across almost every med spa on Instagram — and the reason consistent posting rarely turns into consistent bookings.

Gap 01 — Universal
No Content Funnel
Why It Matters
Posting without a TOFU/MOFU/BOFU structure attracts attention but doesn't convert it. Reach without direction is just noise — and noise doesn't fill a booking calendar.
The Fix
Map every post to a funnel stage — awareness, education, or action — so your content compounds instead of just accumulates. Every post should have a job.
Gap 02 — Universal
No Social SEO
Why It Matters
Instagram is a search engine. Patients search for injectors and med spas in their city. Without keywords in your bio, captions, and Reel audio, you're invisible to the exact people you want to reach.
The Fix
Build a keyword bank from your ideal patient's search behavior and embed it in every post. Your bio, captions, and Reel audio should all be optimized for how patients actually search.
Gap 03 — Med Spa Specific
The "Before & After" Trap
Why It Matters
Most med spas rely entirely on before-and-after photos without explaining the clinical rationale, the patient's anatomy, or the "why" behind the treatment plan. It commoditizes the service and trains patients to shop for results rather than trusting a provider's expertise.
The Fix
Pair every result with the clinical story behind it. What was the anatomical problem? What was the treatment logic? What would have gone wrong with a different approach? That context is what separates a provider from a technician.
Gap 04 — Med Spa Specific
The Discount Spiral
Why It Matters
Using promotions, flash sales, and "tox specials" as the primary hook attracts transactional, price-shopping patients who will leave you for a cheaper injector down the street. It also devalues your expertise and erodes your positioning.
The Fix
Authority-driven content attracts high-ticket, loyal patients who respect your clinical boundaries. Build a content system around your expertise, not your pricing. The right patients don't shop on price — they shop on trust.
Gap 05 — Med Spa Specific
No IVP (Ideal Viewer Profile)
Why It Matters
Without a clear picture of who you're talking to — first-time Botox patient, filler-curious 30-something, post-baby body contouring client, or aging gracefully 50+ patient — your content appeals to everyone and converts no one. Generic content gets generic results.
The Fix
Define your IVP before writing a single caption. Every post should feel like it was written for one specific person — the person most likely to book a consultation. When you talk to everyone, you reach no one.

Where to Embed Keywords

👤
Name Field
The single most important SEO field on your profile. Instagram's search algorithm indexes this first. Include your name, specialty, and city.
"Sarah Jenkins | Chicago Injector & Aesthetics"
✍️
Bio Copy
Weave 2–3 high-intent keywords naturally into your bio. Don't keyword-stuff — write for humans first, search second.
"Helping patients achieve natural, undetectable results through clinical precision and anatomical expertise"
📝
Caption First Line
Instagram indexes the first 125 characters of your caption. Lead with your keyword-rich hook — it's both SEO and the hook that stops the scroll.
"If you're considering Botox for the first time, here's what your injector should tell you before you sit in the chair."
🎙️
Reel Audio (Voiceover)
Instagram auto-transcribes Reel audio and uses it for search indexing. Say your keywords out loud in your Reels — especially in the first 5 seconds.
Open with: "If you're looking for a med spa in [city], here's what separates a great injector from a dangerous one..."
#️⃣
Hashtags (Strategic, Not Spammy)
Use 5–8 targeted hashtags per post. Mix broad, niche, and hyper-local. Avoid generic hashtags with 50M+ posts — you'll never surface there.
#ChicagoMedSpa #ChicagoInjector #BotoxChicago #LipFillerChicago #MedSpaChicago
📍
Location Tags
Tag your location on every post and Reel. Neighborhood-level tags outperform city-level tags for local discovery. Patients searching for a nearby injector will find you first.
Tag your neighborhood or district, not just the city. "Lincoln Park, Chicago" beats "Chicago, Illinois" for local patient searches.
Chapter 04

Content Ideas

Every post should have a job. These ideas are mapped to the funnel stage they serve — awareness, education, or conversion — so you're not just posting, you're building a system.

What Happens to Your Face in Your 30s
TOFU Reel
"Nobody talks about the fact that your face starts changing at 28 — here's what's actually happening under the skin."
Why It Works
Addresses a universal patient concern before they're ready to book. Builds awareness and positions you as the clinical authority who understands aging — not just injectables.
Content Direction
Use a face anatomy diagram or a side-by-side of facial changes over time. Explain collagen loss, fat pad migration, and bone resorption in plain language. No jargon.
Sammy Jones Script Framework
1
Hook (0–3s)
"Nobody tells you that your face starts aging in your late 20s — here's what's actually happening."
2
Relate (3–8s)
"You've probably noticed subtle changes — a little less volume here, a line that wasn't there before. That's not just 'getting older.' There's a clinical reason for it."
3
Educate (8–25s)
Walk through the three layers: collagen loss, fat pad migration, bone resorption. Use a face model or diagram. Keep it visual.
4
CTA (25–30s)
"Understanding how your face ages is the first step to treating it correctly. Save this — and DM me if you want to know what I'd actually recommend for your anatomy."
5 Things That Make a Great Injector
TOFU Carousel
"There are 10,000 injectors in this city. Here's how to tell the difference between someone who's safe and someone who's exceptional."
Why It Works
Educates patients on what to look for — and positions you as the provider who meets every standard. This is authority content that pre-qualifies your ideal patient.
Content Direction
5-slide carousel: anatomical knowledge, individualized treatment planning, safety protocols, honest consultation, and ongoing education. Each slide = one criterion with a brief explanation.
Hot Take: Botox Is Not a One-Size-Fits-All Treatment
TOFU Reel
"If your injector is using the same Botox pattern on every patient, that's a red flag — not a standard of care."
Why It Works
Opinion-led content drives shares and saves. This positions you as the clinical authority who individualized treatment — vs. the cookie-cutter injector down the street.
Content Direction
Explain why anatomy varies (brow position, muscle mass, facial asymmetry) and how that changes your injection approach. Show a real example if possible.
Filler vs. Botox: What's the Actual Difference?
MOFU Carousel
"I get this question every single day. Here's the honest answer — and why it matters for your treatment plan."
Why It Works
This is the #1 question first-time patients Google. Answering it with clinical authority builds trust and pre-educates your ideal patient before they even book a consultation.
Content Direction
Side-by-side comparison: mechanism of action, target areas, duration, cost, and ideal candidate. End with: "The right answer depends on your anatomy and goals — not a menu."
What I Look for in a Consultation
MOFU Reel
"Before I ever pick up a needle, here are the 5 things I assess in every new patient consultation."
Why It Works
Demystifies the consultation process and reduces the anxiety that stops first-time patients from booking. Shows your clinical rigor before they ever walk in the door.
Content Direction
Walk through your actual assessment: facial symmetry, skin quality, bone structure, muscle movement, patient goals. Film it in your treatment room for authenticity.

3

Walk through your 5 assessment criteria. Be specific. Use clinical language but explain it in plain terms.
4
CTA (25–30s)
"If you want a consultation that actually looks at your face — not just your wish list — DM me 'CONSULT' and I'll send you my booking link."
The Anatomy Behind Lip Filler Gone Wrong
MOFU Reel
"The 'duck lip' isn't just bad aesthetics — it's an anatomy problem. Here's what actually causes it."
Why It Works
Addresses the #1 fear patients have about lip filler. Answering this clinically builds enormous trust and positions you as the provider who won't make that mistake.
Content Direction
Use a face model or diagram to show the lip anatomy. Explain the philtrum columns, vermillion border, and cupid's bow. Show what over-projection looks like vs. natural enhancement.
Patient Case Study: Natural Jawline Refinement
BOFU Carousel
"She came in wanting a 'sharper jawline.' Here's what I actually did — and why it's not what she expected."
Why It Works
Case studies are the highest-converting content type for med spas. They show your clinical thinking, your results, and your patient relationship — all in one post.
Content Direction
Before/after + clinical narrative: patient's concern, your assessment, your treatment rationale, the outcome, and the patient's feedback. Never just post a before/after without the story.

Removes the anxiety barrier that stops first-time patients from booking. When they know what to expect, they're far more likely to take the next step.
Content Direction
Walk-through of your actual consultation process: intake, assessment, discussion, treatment options, no-pressure decision. Film in your actual space. Authenticity is the point.

Patient Testimonial: "I Was Terrified of Looking Fake"

"She almost didn't book because she was scared of looking overdone. Here's what happened instead."

Social proof from a patient who had the same fear as your ideal viewer is the most powerful conversion tool in your arsenal. It's not a testimonial — it's a mirror.
Content Direction
Patient-led video or quote carousel. Let them describe their fear, their experience, and their result in their own words. Your job is to frame it, not narrate it.
Chapter 05

The CTA System

A call-to-action is not a command. It's an invitation. The best CTAs in med spa content feel like a natural next step — not a sales pitch. Here's how to build a system that converts at every stage of the funnel.

"The best CTA doesn't feel like marketing. It feels like the most logical next step for someone who just learned something valuable."
🎯
One CTA per post. Multiple asks = zero action. Pick one thing you want them to do and make it obvious.
📍
Match the CTA to the funnel stage. A TOFU post should ask for a save or follow — not a booking. Asking for too much too soon kills conversion.
🔑
Use keyword CTAs. "DM me 'CONSULT'" triggers Instagram's automation tools and creates a trackable, repeatable lead gen system.
TOFU — Awareness
Save & Share CTA
For educational content. The goal is reach and retention — not bookings. Ask them to save it for later or share it with someone who needs it.
Example
"Save this before your next consultation — you'll want to reference it."
TOFU — Awareness
Follow CTA
For broad-reach content. The goal is to convert a new viewer into a follower — the first step in your trust-building funnel.
Example
"Follow for more clinical education on what actually works — and what doesn't."
MOFU — Education
DM Keyword CTA
For mid-funnel content. Triggers an automated DM sequence. Delivers a lead magnet, a guide, or a direct booking link in exchange for engagement.
Example
"DM me 'GUIDE' and I'll send you my free First-Time Botox Patient Checklist."
MOFU — Education
Comment Trigger CTA
Drives comment engagement (which boosts reach) while delivering value. Works well on educational carousels and myth-busting content.
Example
"Comment 'ANATOMY' and I'll send you the full breakdown of how facial aging actually works."
BOFU — Conversion
Booking CTA
For high-intent content — case studies, testimonials, before/afters with clinical context. Direct ask for a consultation or appointment.
Example
"If you've been thinking about it — link in bio to book your comprehensive facial assessment."
BOFU — Conversion
Consultation CTA
Lower-friction than a direct booking ask. Positions the consultation as a conversation, not a commitment — ideal for hesitant first-time patients.
Example
"DM me 'CONSULT' — no pressure, just a conversation about what's right for your face."
The Link in Bio System
Your link in bio is the most important conversion tool on your profile. Most med spas waste it. Here's how to use it correctly.
✗
Don't use a Linktree with 8 options. Every option you add reduces the chance they click any of them. Decision fatigue is real — and it's killing your conversion rate.
✗
Don't link to your homepage. A homepage is a starting point, not a destination. Visitors who land on a homepage have to figure out what to do next — most won't.
✓
Link to one specific destination that matches your primary CTA for the month. If your CTA is "Book a consultation," your link should go directly to your booking page — one click, no friction.
✓
Change your link monthly. Align it with your primary business goal for the month — new patient consultations, a specific treatment promotion, or a lead magnet. Update your bio copy to match.
✓
Track link taps. Link taps are the most important conversion metric on Instagram. They tell you how many people took action — not just how many people watched.

Lead Magnets

The best lead magnets don't feel like marketing — they feel like the most useful thing a patient has ever gotten for free. Here's how to build one that actually converts followers into consultations.

A lead magnet is not a PDF you made in Canva. It's a bridge between a stranger's attention and a clinical conversation.

The best lead magnets for med spas solve a specific, high-stakes problem that your ideal patient is already searching for — before they're ready to book. They provide real value, build trust, and end with a clear, natural next step.

Generic lead magnets ("10 Skincare Tips") get downloaded and forgotten. Specific lead magnets ("The First-Time Botox Patient Checklist: 8 Questions to Ask Before You Sit in the Chair") get saved, shared, and acted on.
